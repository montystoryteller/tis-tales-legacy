Fate Third weekend of July

There are four main tents :  Main tent seats about 200 high stage, amplification, lights on stage and well dressed. Stage at centre of long side and chairs arranged in arc

Red tent:  small low dais simple amplification, sits about 80

Yellow tent:  for workshops easily fits 40

Children’s tent fit 40.  No stage, no focus.  Had to create a corner using carpet, bits of material and elephant wall hanging lots of bunting,  craft workshops very well attended.

Beer tent, no stage, no amplification for spontaneous music jamming

No panic circus tent

Overall very traditional storytelling.  No modern, anecdotal, nor based on local history

No factual talks – except Angeline Morrison.  Stood out as breath of fresh air

Not many workshops eg voice, basketry, make a cushion

A lot of dragons and impossible situations

Cultural Diversity:  tom van outre,  Xanthe telling arabian(?) tales; Peter Chand

No signing, no obviously disabled performers,

Very well organised, good facilities,  plenty of loos, four showers, four food outlets,

Merchandise tent/box office

First aid tent

Fire extinguishers at each tent.

Flags around site and along periphery looked great.

Road arrows simple but distinctive use of festival colours

About 6 stalls selling festival garb/ trinkets/hats
