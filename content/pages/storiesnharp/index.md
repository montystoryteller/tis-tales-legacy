## Repertoire

_**Gwion Bach**_ — traditional Welsh tale, the origin story of Taliesin, greatest of the bards \[ [storynotes](https://psychemedia.github.io/storytelling-notes/gwion-bach.html)\]. Music: _Lavaromp Ar Chapelet_, arr. Dominic Bouchard. [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fI6W8YiLF2I)

**_Binnorie / The Twa Sisters_** — traditional Scottish / Northern English / Scandinavian ballad, of jealousy, murder and singing bones, based on _cante fable_ version in Joseph Jacobs' _English Fairy Tales_, 1890 \[ [storynotes](https://psychemedia.github.io/storytelling-notes/twa-sisters-binnorie.html)\]. Lyrics: Child, version C. [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLBBg4MjO6o). [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OyYzXh9RxBc), [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=SlB_QPMUWmg)

**_Oisin and Tir na Nog_** — _Oisin and the Land of the Ever Young_, traditional Irish tale, a basis story for how we come to know the tales of Fionn MacCumhail and the Fianna \[ [storynotes](https://psychemedia.github.io/finn-resources/oisin-tir-na-nog.html)\]. Music: _La Gueussinette_ (Steve Jones, arr. Ailie Robertson). [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=slgwELi0B2o)

**_The Pedlar of Swaffham_** — traditional English story \[ [_draft_ storynotes](https://github.com/psychemedia/storytelling-notes/blob/main/pedlar-of-swaffham.md)\]. Music: _Apple Tree Wassail_, trad., arranged by Fran Barsby. Lyrics: version by Doug Eunson and Sarah Mathews, additional lyrics by Jenni Charity. [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v0FMpKUF-3A)

_**Sir Orfeo / Sir Orpheo**_ — traditional English romance, based on the classical myth of Orpheus and Eurydice \[ [_draft_ storynotes](https://github.com/psychemedia/middle-english-romances/blob/main/sir-orfeo-telling.md)\]. Music: _Crossing the Bar_, composed by Theresa Ellis; _Tristan and Yseult_, composed by Hervé Quefféléant. [🎧](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=inX9QRj7Meo)

_**Mabinogion Second Branch / Bran and Branwen**_ — Traditional Welsh story, a roller-coaster of a ride from the marriage of Branwen, to the pillage of Ireland, to a legend of the Tower of London \[ [storynotes](https://psychemedia.github.io/storytelling-notes/bendigeidfran_branwen_second_branch.html)\]. Music: _to follow..._.
